As a designer,

I swear to fulfill, to the best of my ability and judgment, this covenant:

Read the code of ethics statement

I will respect the freedom of all human-beings, I will not force, deceive, nor lure users to take actions they are not intended to take.

Using the concepts from Libertarianism, we should always value the freedom of our users, and we should never force them to do anything, even the good things.

I will remember that I as a designer, care about the users as ends themselves, users’ experience as users’ experience. I will never see my users as means to get paid, or to recieve benefits of any other personal interests.

Based on the Deontology idea, we are moral when we act within a categorical imperative, which “by itself commands absolutely and without any further motives. As designers, we should act within the motive of helping our users.

I will prevent design decisions that will harm people whenever I can, for prevention is preferable to reaction.

Because putting Utilitarianism in perspective, good design decisions should maximize society’s happiness instead of pain.

I will not hesitate to say my concerns about a design out loud, to say no to decisions I don’t agree with my heart, nor will I fail to admit my shorts and ask for help to achieve the best solutions.

According to Virtue Ethics, good designers should do the things that make them good designers. And good designers should not hide their worries or afraid of admit their problems. They should care about producing the best quality design, and do the things they need to do to achieve that.

I will make sure my design can do what it supposes to do. My apps will function like good apps should function, and my websites will function like good websites should function. I will make sure my users receive what they deserve and being treated as they deserved.

In Virtue Ethics, we have the idea of telos and give people what they deserved. As designers, we should design things that meet the purpose of the design, demonstrate all the telos, and also reward users morally, which means as they deserved.

I will remember, that my design should help to make the world a better place, to bring happiness to the world.

Because from the Utilitarianism standpoint, the highest principle of morality is to maximize happiness in the world.

I will keep in mind that even when I’m design for a tiny little problem, my decision will affect populations beyond just a small group. My responsibility includes their families, social groups, communities, and even cities, countries and the world. There is no small problems, and I will always be aware of the consequences of my choice.

Learned from the Utilitarianism concepts, that we should always care about consequences. And consequences are about the whole world, a way larger picture than our user group.

I will stay humble and keep learning. I will respect and love my colleges, give them credits for their contribution and encourage them when they fail. I will give honest feedback when asked, whether it’s good or bad. I will not take critiques and rejections personal, nor will I give personal, non-professional critiques or rejections.

All these things are qualities that a good designer need to have to be a good designer. And to be a good designer, using the Virtue Ethics, we should do what good designer ought to do.

Principle

Concrete Rules to Follow

To respect the freedom of all human-beings, I should...

  • Give the users freedom to customerize
  • Allow the users to say no to requirments and conditions
  • Be clear about policies and terms and let the users decide

To treat users as ends themselves, I should...

  • Design with intentions that cares about the users
  • Do not make design decisions with the intention of earning personal benefits
  • Love design itself, and make this the motive of creation

To prevent harmful decisions, I should...

  • Think about possible concequences while making decisions
  • Take all affected parties into consideration
  • Be calme and be brave

To achieve the best solutions, I should...

  • Be vocally active about thoughts and opinions
  • Not be afraid of saying no and rejecting others
  • Do not hide imperfections and flaws of designs

To make sure a design can do what it supposes to do, I should...

  • Be very clear about the purpose of the design
  • Make sure all requirments are met during the process
  • Make sure the system is rewarding the right people and promoting wanted actions

To make the world a better place, I should...

  • Do research and be clear about people's needs
  • Make sure my design can benefit people and help people
  • Keep finding solutions that can maximize the good effect

To be aware of the consequences of my choices, I should...

  • List all the stateholders and people/things that might be affected
  • Be aware of how them might be affected by this choice I am making
  • Balance the pros and cons everytime

To do the things good designers do, I should...

  • Be professional in work, do not put personal feelings into the workspace
  • Always be ready for failures, and have the courage to be back
  • Never stop learning
Activity

Code of Ethics' Tarot of Answers

Think about your problem and chose a tarot with your instinct...

“What would a great designer do?” Do you have a designer role model? What would he/she do in the situation? Do the things that every great designer should do.

“What brings greater happiness to the world?” Value all the possible good and bad concequences, and find a balanced plan.

“Who will be affected by my choice?” List all the possible stateholders. Are you rescpect and consider all of them?

“Good choices come from good intentions.” Why are you making the desicions you are making? Are these intentions good?

“What is the purpose of the thing you are designing?” What goal should it achieve? What should it be like? How to make the design meet your purpose?

“Will this decision harm anyone?” Consider all possible outcomes before you choose

“Are you forcing/making my users to do anything?” Your users are free human-beings and they should make their own decisions

“If you have worries, say it out loud and ask for help.” Nothing is more valuable than the best design you can create. Don't be afraid.
